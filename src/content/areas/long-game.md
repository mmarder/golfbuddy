## Clubs

Rule of thumb: about **10 yards between consecutive clubs** — a higher number flies shorter and a little higher (a 9-iron carries shorter and higher than a 7-iron).

- **Driver** — off the tee on longer holes, once your 7-iron contact is reliable. Most distance, least forgiving — smooth tempo beats swinging hard.
- **Hybrid 4** — your go-to long club. Off the tee when the driver feels risky, and for long approaches. Much easier to hit than a long iron.
- **7-iron** — mid approaches, and the club to learn your swing with. Most of your range time.
- **9-iron** — shorter approaches; flies higher and stops quicker.

## Setup by club

Rule of thumb: the lower the club number (the longer the club), the **wider** the stance and the further **forward** the ball.

| Club | Stance width | Weight (front / back) | Ball position |
|---|---|---|---|
| Driver | Widest | Stays back, behind the ball | Off the front foot |
| Hybrid 4 | Wide | 20 / 80 | Forward of centre |
| 7-iron | Standard | 40 / 60 | Centre |

## Lesson learnings

**Backswing**

- Eyes stay on the ball.
- Turn through the shoulders.
- Left arm straight, right arm folds.

> ⚠️ **Watch-out:** don't let the club go too far behind your back at the top.

> ⚠️ **Watch-out:** extend the arms on both the backswing and the through-swing — keep your width; don't let the arms collapse or chicken-wing.

**Downswing / through**

- Turn through the hips and turn the body.
- Weight moves from the back foot to the front foot; the back foot turns up onto the toes.

> ⚠️ **Watch-out:** stay down in your posture — don't push up onto your toes and lose your height during the swing. The trail foot rolling up in the follow-through is fine; your whole body rising is not.

**Driver**

- Wider stance, ball off the front foot (see Setup by club).
- Through the ball, let the club release — the forearms rotate and the clubhead folds over past impact.

**How to practice:** start with half swings, then build to full swings.
